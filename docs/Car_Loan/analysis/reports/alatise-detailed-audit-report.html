Executive Summary

This report documents the reconciliation of 51 monthly payroll-deduction records attributable to one beneficiary (Alatise / Alatishe Bosede Susainah) against the Oyo State Car Loan Scheme's standard computation formula (Principal × 13.33% over a 60-month base, regardless of chosen tenure). The records span 38 months across two Ministries, Departments and Agencies and decompose into four distinct loan signatures.

Multiple data quality observations were identified across all four loan signatures, including a tenure misrecording with a lifetime variance of approximately ₦320,656 against scheme expected, an arithmetic inconsistency in the installments-remaining column inflated by exactly 20 across 25 consecutive monthly records, and a frozen-template pattern in which a single row replicates unchanged across 13 monthly files. The cross-MDA presence of the same beneficiary in two Ministries simultaneously is currently indistinguishable, by data alone, from a transferred employee, a parallel loan, or a clerical error.

These observations are presented for review. Each would be detectable by a production-grade Identity, Variance and Lifecycle reconciliation engine. None are currently surfaced by the in-flight VLPRS pipeline at the portfolio level. The pattern is unlikely to be unique to this beneficiary and is expected to repeat across the 4,926-strong scheme population at material rates.

Why four loan signatures matter

Today's pipeline assigns staff identifier and loan identifier per upload. Cross-month tracking, loan completion detection and variance attribution all become unreliable. Each of the four signatures is examined individually below.

Cross-cutting observations and recommendations

Cross-cutting observations

Cross-MDA presence without Transfer Handshake

Group 3 (Board of Internal Revenue, ₦450,000, 30-month) and Group 4 (Civil Service Commission, ₦450,000, 36-month declared) overlap in time from January 2025 through February 2026. Three readings are possible: (a) the beneficiary transferred from BIR to CSC and the BIR file continued to include her erroneously; (b) the beneficiary holds two concurrent loans across two MDAs, which would require explicit Scheme Secretariat clarification of whether concurrent loans are permitted; or (c) one of the two MDAs is filing the wrong beneficiary or a stale row. The data alone cannot distinguish these. Resolution requires either a Transfer Handshake event filed by both MDAs, an authoritative scheme rule on concurrent loans, or direct enquiry with both MDAs.

Lifecycle inference from absence

Group 1 (BIR, ₦750,000) reaches the declared tenure of 28 months by December 2023 and disappears from subsequent BIR files. No explicit loan-completion event is recorded. The system has no observable means to distinguish (a) normal completion, (b) accelerated payoff (Settlement Pathway 2), (c) walk-up lump-sum settlement at the Car Loan Department (Settlement Pathway 3), (d) gratuity-deduct on retirement (Settlement Pathway 4), or (e) MDA simply ceasing to report this beneficiary. Each pathway has different financial implications for AG. Without explicit settlement events, the loan-end is recorded as inference, not fact.

Variance engine versus dashboard total

The Variance Engine in the in-flight pipeline correctly computes the per-record variance for Group 1 (lifetime approximately ₦320,656 against scheme expected). However, today's dashboard aggregations sum MDA-declared values without gating by variance status. A record with a known variance contributes to AG headline figures (Active Loans, Total Exposure, Loans in Window) at face value. The gap between "MDA declared" and "Reconciled" is therefore not visible to AG today.

Population scaling

A single beneficiary produced eight distinct observations of varying severity. The 4,926-strong scheme population is unlikely to be uniformly clean. Even at modest contamination rates, the cumulative impact on portfolio totals is material. Empirical characterisation across the full population is recommended as a priority discovery activity prior to the publication of authoritative figures.

Recommendation

That a structured Identity, Continuity and Reconciliation track be commissioned, comprising a discovery spike against the existing 4,926-record population and a phased build of three coupled engine gates (Identity, Variance, Lifecycle) plus a Person Reconciliation Pass for set-based, order-independent ingestion. Initial pilot is recommended against a single cooperative MDA (Board of Internal Revenue is the natural candidate given existing data depth and engagement). Authoritative dashboard figures should adopt a dual-truth presentation (Reconciled / Pending Review) to surface data quality honestly to AG and beneficiaries.

That production go-live of VLPRS authoritative figures be paused pending completion of the discovery spike and the first pilot cycle. Existing infrastructure (in-flight upload, exception, reporting and certificate workflows) should remain available in non-authoritative mode for continued operational use and engagement.

That Settlement Pathway 3 (walk-up lump-sum settlement) be added as a first-class event with explicit Car Loan Department officer filing, dual-signature requirement above a threshold, and bank-receipt cross-reference, to close the current observability gap and the associated fraud surface.
