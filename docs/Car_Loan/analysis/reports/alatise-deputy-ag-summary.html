The observation in three lines

One beneficiary appears in side-quest data under two name spellings, in two MDAs, with four distinct loan signatures. Today's pipeline would treat her as four separate beneficiaries with four loans; the Variance Engine would correctly compute per-record variances but would not gate AG dashboard totals. What this means at population scale
  • One beneficiary produced eight distinct observations across four loan signatures. The 4,926-beneficiary scheme population is not expected to be uniformly clean. Even modest contamination rates are material at portfolio level.
  • Today's authoritative figures (Active Loans, Total Exposure, Loans in Window) sum MDA-declared values without gating by reconciliation status. The gap between MDA-declared and reconciled is not visible to AG.
  • Settlement Pathway 3 (walk-up lump-sum payoff at the Car Loan Department) has no first-class event in the system today. Such loans appear as "stalled" indistinguishably from MDAs that have stopped reporting a beneficiary, transferred without handshake, or not filed a death or retirement event.
Recommendation for the Deputy AG's consideration

(a) Pause production go-live of VLPRS authoritative figures pending completion of an Identity, Continuity and Reconciliation discovery spike and a first pilot cycle. (b) Commission the Identity, Continuity and Reconciliation engineering track (three coupled gates: Identity, Variance, Lifecycle; plus a Person Reconciliation Pass for order-independent ingestion). (c) Begin pilot with the Board of Internal Revenue, the MDA with the deepest existing data engagement. (d) Add Settlement Pathway 3 as a first-class event with explicit Car Loan Department officer filing and bank-receipt cross-reference. (e) Adopt dual-truth dashboard presentation (Reconciled / Pending Review) so AG can read both the current and the reconciled portfolio at a glance. The detailed audit report (separately attached) documents the per-record evidence supporting these recommendations.
