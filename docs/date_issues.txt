  The Collection Design Decision

  John sees three realistic approaches. Let's think through each:

  Approach A: Expand the Monthly CSV (7 or 8 Fields)

  Add Event Date as a 7th field. When Event Flag = NONE, Event Date is blank. When Event Flag = RETIRED, Event Date = the effective retirement    
  date.

  Pros:
  - Minimal training change â€” MDA officers already know the CSV flow
  - Single submission channel â€” no new interface to learn
  - Event date rides alongside the event flag naturally

  Cons:
  - Event reporting is now tied to the monthly submission cycle. If someone dies on the 2nd and the submission isn't due until the 28th, the event
   waits 26 days
  - Doesn't solve the static profile data (DOB, appointment date) â€” that's not a monthly field

  Approach B: Two-Track Collection

  Track 1 â€” Staff Profile (once, at migration or loan onboarding):
  - Department Admin (Mr. Okonkwo) enters DOB and first appointment date for each staff member
  - During migration: added to the migration spreadsheet as required columns
  - For new loans post-MVP: captured during loan setup
  - Retirement date computed automatically by the system

  Track 2 â€” Employment Event Report (separate from monthly submission):
  - A simple form: Staff ID, Event Type, Effective Date, Supporting Reference (letter number, etc.), Notes
  - Available to MDA Reporting Officers anytime â€” not tied to the monthly cycle
  - Department Admin can also file events (since they're the ones who often get the call)

  Monthly CSV stays at 6 fields. Clean, simple, unchanged.

  Pros:
  - MDA officers' monthly workflow stays simple â€” the 6-field win is preserved
  - Events can be reported AS THEY HAPPEN, not on a monthly cycle
  - Profile data is collected once properly, not repeated monthly
  - Separation of concerns â€” facts vs events

  Cons:
  - Two interfaces for MDA officers to learn (monthly submission + event reporting)
  - Risk that MDA officers forget to report events separately

  Approach C: Hybrid â€” Expand CSV + Separate Event Form

  CSV becomes 7 fields (add Event Date). BUT also provide a standalone event reporting form for mid-cycle events. The monthly submission catches  
  anything the standalone form missed.

  Pros:
  - Belt and suspenders â€” events can be reported immediately OR caught in the monthly cycle
  - If the standalone form is skipped, the CSV is the safety net

  Cons:
  - Potential duplicate reporting (event filed via form AND via CSV)
  - More complexity

  John's Recommendation: Approach B (Two-Track) â€” Here's WHY

  The 6-field monthly CSV is one of VLPRS's greatest design wins. It's what takes Mrs. Adebayo from 3 hours to 12 minutes. John does NOT want to  
  compromise that simplicity. Every field you add to the monthly submission increases friction for 63 officers, 12 months a year.

  Employment events are episodic, not periodic. A retirement happens once. A death happens once. These are not monthly rhythms â€” they're
  interruptions. They deserve their own simple, purpose-built channel.

  Here's what John proposes:

  Staff Profile Data (Track 1):
  - During migration: Mr. Okonkwo adds DOB and date of first appointment to the migration spreadsheet (he has access to personnel files)
  - For new loans: Committee approval workflow requires DOB and appointment date before loan can be created
  - System computes retirement date automatically
  - Department Admin can correct if needed (e.g., approved service extension)

  Employment Event Reporting (Track 2):
  - Simple form available to MDA Reporting Officers and Department Admin
  - 5 fields: Staff ID, Event Type (dropdown), Effective Date, Reference Number (optional), Notes (optional)
  - Available anytime â€” not tied to submission cycle
  - System sends confirmation: "Event recorded for Staff ID XXXX. Loan status updated."

  Monthly CSV stays at 6 fields. But the system cross-checks: if the monthly submission shows zero deduction for a staff member AND no employment 
  event has been filed, the system flags it as "Missing Event â€” possible unreported status change." This is your safety net.
